The Solution

Using what we have learned in "the process" we came up with a solution using Hub and Expereince Builder to meet our proposed goals. Through the hub we are able to provide an engaging platform that informs the community on the importance of shorelines and encourages involvement. Using experience builder we were able to further communicate shoreline importance, and provide users the oppurtunity to explore the digitization process. Using these two web solutions we believe we have realized our goal of creating an application that can be used by the community and municipal groups alike, to help make informed decisions on how to manage shoreline development in the future.

ArcGIS Hub

ArcGIS hub was used to create a website to host the final mapping program. These webpages were created with community members in mind and include a variety of different links and resources that to further inform community members and enhance community support for the shoreline conservation. The website was broken into 3 pages. A landing page for Natural Shoreline Solutions (our team name), which describes goals of our organization, and the groups that we are partnered with. The second page decribes Shoreline Importance to further inform individuals about shorelines and their important ecosystem function, as well as how to conserve and protect these riparian areas. The third page goes into detail on the Lake Simcoe Shoreline digitization project and provides the link to the mapping application hosted on the expereince builder.

Expereince Builder

The ESRI experience builder furthered our project by adding an interactive map component that can be used to further inform citizens on the scope of the project. This tool could be used by community members to learn about each of the digitization classifications involved, if One wanted to learn about the technicalities of the project, or could be used for self interest by property owners to see how their shoreline was digitized.
